Passwords
Although encryption isn't something everyone thinks about when they are asked to set up a password for their accounts it is key to the security of their information. With this tool we have endeavored to teach the public a little about a few different types of encryption and how/why password security is so importent.
Here are a few tips to make you password more secure:
  1. Make sure you use different passwords for each of your accounts. This can be difficult but use a password manager to help keep yourself secure.
  2. Be sure no one watches when you enter your password.
  3. Always log off if you leave your device, it only takes a moment for someone to steal or change the password.
  4. Avoid entering passwords on computers you don't control, they may have malware that could steal your passwords.
  5. Avoid entering passwords when using unsecured Wi-Fi connections (like at the airport, school or coffee shop), hackers can intercept your passwords using free software like Wireshark.
  6. Don't tell anyone your password. Your trusted friend now might not be your friend in the future. Keep your passwords safe by keeping them to yourself.
  7. Depending on the sensitivity of the information being protected, you should change your passwords periodically, and avoid reusing a password for at least one year.
  8. Do use at least eight characters of lowercase and uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols in your password. Remember, the more the merrier.
  9. Strong passwords are easy to remember but hard to guess. Iam:)2b29!, This has 10 characters and says "I am happy to be 29!" I wish.
  10. Have fun with known short codes or sentences or phrases. 2B-or-Not_2b?, This one says "To be or not to be?"
  11. It's okay to write down your passwords, just keep them away from your computer and mixed in with other numbers and letters so it's not apparent that it's a password.
  12. You can also write a "tip sheet" which will give you a clue to remember your password, but doesn't actually contain your password on it. For example, in the example above, your "tip sheet" might read "To be, or not to be?"
